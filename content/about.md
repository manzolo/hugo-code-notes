# About me 👋

![manzolo](/img/manzolo.jpeg)

I’m a person constantly striving to improve myself and the world around me. I first discovered computing as a child through video games, and what began as play soon turned into curiosity about the “magic” behind how things worked. I grew up on Windows 95, then Windows NT and 2000, before Linux won me over — the system I still work and play with today.

Professionally, I started with client–server languages and moved toward web programming; building **REST APIs** to make different systems talk to each other is one of the things I do. Today, as an **IT technician at CNR-IFAC** (a public research institute in Florence), together with two colleagues I run a **mid-sized datacenter** — systems administration, infrastructure and services.

At home I keep the same passion alive with my **homelab** — what I like to call "a small server," which is really a **Proxmox** host packed with VMs and containers: self-hosted services, **Home Assistant** and home automation, a NAS, **local-AI** experiments, and even the sensor data from my camper (battery, GPS, temperature). All held together with **Docker**, **Git** and plenty of curiosity.

More recently I've caught the bug for repairing electrical and electronic gear — thanks to **[Pier Aisa](https://www.youtube.com/@pieraisa)**'s YouTube channel — and it's also what nudged me toward the ham radio licence. I've set up a small bench (a few multimeters, a simple oscilloscope, a variac, three bench power supplies, a basic signal generator) and tasted the real joy of swapping a bulging capacitor and watching a board spring back to life… along with the deep frustration of failing at things I'd assumed were easy. Either way, picking up the soldering iron to restore a broken trace makes me happy. That's what got me an **Arduino** starter kit to learn how it works, plus tinkering with **Raspberry Pi 3 and 4** (not the 5 yet — I don't see the leap for me).

In my free time I build open-source tools and tinker endlessly; lately a lot of my day-to-day is AI coding agents like **Claude** and **Codex**. Beyond code: I recently earned my ham radio licence (**IU5VMO**) — the station itself is still to be set up — and I love writing songs, mostly for myself, with [Suno](https://suno.com/@manzolo).

I don’t hold a university degree, and I never chased certifications — they simply weren’t my path. I’m happy with the know-how I built hands-on, one step at a time, and that’s what represents me best.

I’ve never wanted to become a specialist in any one thing — it might have brought more career and more money, but I’d rather stay an “absolute beginner” and often start over from scratch to build a new path. On the scale, curiosity has always outweighed the wallet.

I’m looking for a dynamic environment that can **channel my inner nerd into what it actually needs** — one that believes in investing in people and knows how to get the best out of me.

Truth be told: one of my defining traits is doing a whole lot of things and never quite managing to sell myself. With this site I'm trying to put a small piece of me "on display" — the very thing I never really pulled off in all these years. 🙂

Welcome to **Code Notes**, my personal corner of the internet where I document my journey through the ever-evolving world of software and systems.
